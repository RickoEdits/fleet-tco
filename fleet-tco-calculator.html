How it's calculated: Finance cost uses a standard reducing-balance loan payment against the residual/balloon value. Fuel, service and tyre costs scale with monthly distance (ending odometer minus starting odometer, ÷ term) — set the starting odometer above 0 if you're buying a vehicle that's already been driven. Insurance is a monthly rate applied to the vehicle's cost. Pricing is indicative ex-showroom South Africa. Fuel consumption is fixed to the manufacturer-cycle figure for each vehicle and can't be edited. Every other field — vehicle cost, RV/balloon, service & maintenance, tyres, licensing and insurance — is a starting estimate you can adjust to match your own fleet's quotes; service rates marked "● matched" come from real cost-per-km fleet data, "○ estimated" ones use a segment-level assumption. Insurance defaults are calibrated against real South African comprehensive premium data (0.30%–0.45% of vehicle value per month, varying by vehicle type), not a flat guess. Every card also shows a cost-per-km (R/km) figure — your total cost over the contract term divided by the distance driven — so new and second-hand vehicles compare on the same basis. Second-hand vehicles: tick "This is a second-hand unit" under "Edit inputs" on any vehicle's card, then enter its purchase price and current mileage — RV, service, tyres, licensing and insurance stay open to adjust to match its condition. Confirm final figures with your financier and dealership before signing.
